Pricing & pilot packages · scope-based

Commercial paths for pilot evaluation, custom integration, and ongoing use.

PayeeProof is a decision layer for transfer safety, so commercial fit depends on workflow scope, review depth, supported networks, and how operationally strict the team needs the decision record to be. The cleanest path is usually a narrow pilot first, then expansion only if the workflow proves useful.

Best starting point

One workflow, one operator pain point, and one clear success criterion. Narrow pilots close faster and produce better review signal.

Commercial principle

Pricing follows scope, policy depth, and rollout complexity rather than a random seat count that says nothing about operational risk.

What buyers get

A defined evaluation path, clear next actions, and a commercial discussion anchored to a real workflow instead of a vague demo impression.

Packages

Three practical commercial paths.

For teams that want to test one narrow flow before committing to deeper integration.

  • Defined networks and assets in scope
  • Review of verdict language and destination classes

Scope drivers

What usually affects pricing and pilot scope.

Operational complexity

  • How many workflows need verification
  • Whether the operator needs strict routing or escalation logic
  • How many destination patterns or exception paths matter
  • Whether the team needs support-style recovery handling too

Coverage and rollout

  • Networks, assets, and destination classes in scope
  • Depth of pilot review and example coverage
  • Email-first review versus implementation-heavy rollout
  • Need for repeated operational use beyond a narrow pilot

Best low-friction path

Start with one workflow and a short success definition. A narrow pilot is usually faster to approve.

Best buyer signal

The strongest commercial conversations happen after buyers review real records, policy examples, trust boundaries, and one scoped workflow.

What not to do

Do not start with a giant “platform rollout” discussion before confirming that the decision layer is valuable in one real operational step.

Commercial model

Commercial terms follow workflow reality, not generic SaaS theater.

No public fixed price yet

At this stage, the more serious approach is to price around a defined workflow, review depth, and rollout path instead of publishing arbitrary numbers too early.

Why that helps buyers

Buyers pay for an operationally useful scope: what gets verified, what gets classified, how records look, and what action the team can take.

What happens next

After a fit review, the commercial discussion can move to a scoped pilot, a custom integration path, or a broader ongoing relationship.

FAQ

Common buyer questions.

Do we need a call before starting?

No. A pilot fit review can start by email if you share the workflow, networks, assets, and the decision you want before approval.

Can we start small?

Yes. The best starting point is one workflow, one problem, and one success criterion. Expansion comes after the pilot proves useful.

Why are public prices not fixed yet?

Because the product is bought as an operational control for a workflow, not as a generic self-serve widget. Scope quality matters more than a decorative price table.

What should we send for a serious review?

Current payout or support flow, networks and assets in scope, destination types, where errors happen today, and what action your team needs from the verdict.
