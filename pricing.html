Packages

Three practical commercial paths.

Pilot

  • One selected payout or approval workflow
  • Defined networks, assets, and destination classes in coverage
  • Pre-send verdicts, records, and proof-style outputs
  • Email-first review with measurable pilot success criteria

Coverage drivers

What usually affects pricing and pilot coverage.

Operational complexity

  • How many workflows need verification
  • Whether the operator needs strict routing or escalation logic
  • How many destination patterns or exception paths matter
  • Whether recovery guidance is needed as a secondary module

Coverage and rollout

  • Networks, assets, and destination classes in coverage
  • Depth of pilot review and example coverage
  • Email-first review versus implementation-heavy rollout
  • Need for repeated operational use beyond a narrow pilot

Best low-friction path

Start with one workflow and a short success definition. A narrow pilot is usually faster to approve.

Best early signal

The strongest commercial conversations happen after teams review real records, policy examples, trust boundaries, and one clearly defined workflow.

What not to do

Do not start with a giant “platform rollout” discussion before confirming that the decision layer is valuable in one real operational step.

Commercial model

Commercial terms now follow one clear sequence.

What is sold first

Pre-send verification is the first commercial package. It is the clearest problem, the easiest pilot entry, and the strongest first proof of value.

What stays secondary

Recovery guidance is available, but it should not dilute the first sale. Add it when the team also owns a real post-error support workflow.

How expansion works

The path is simple: Pilot first, Growth after proof, Enterprise only when wider rollout is justified.

FAQ

Common questions.

Do we need a call before starting?

No. A pilot fit review can start by email if you share the workflow, networks, assets, and the decision you want before approval.

Can we start small?

Yes. The best starting point is one workflow, one problem, and one success criterion. Expansion comes after the pilot proves useful.

Is recovery part of the first package?

No. The first package should stay anchored to pre-send verification. Recovery can be added later if the team also needs post-error support handling.

What should we send for a serious review?

Current payout or support flow, networks and assets in coverage, destination types, where errors happen today, and what action your team needs from the verdict.
